claims of militia links rock colombian presidency crisis for uribe as senator admits paramilitary pact more key allies face top court inquiry over claims sibylla brodzinsky in bogotá thursday november 30 2006 guardian colombias political establishment has been shaken by neardaily allegations of how president álvaro uribes allies worked with rightwing militias who for more than decade used terror in pursuit of their own ends the supreme court has ordered six prouribe lawmakers including the foreign ministers brother to answer questions about alleged links with the paramilitaries who are blamed for the murder and torture of thousands of colombians two senators an acting representative and former congresswoman from the northern coastal province of sucre have been arrested while one former governor remains at large senator álvaro garcía the grandfather of sucre politics is facing charges of murder for allegedly planning the killing of 14 villagers in 2000 and ordering the murder of an election agency official political analyst vicente torrijos from rosario university in the capital bogotá said the claims could have domino effect on the establishment as politicians implicate others to save themselves senator miguel espriella from northern córdoba province revealed in sunday newspaper that he was among some 40 politicians who signed political pact with paramilitary leaders at the height of their power in 2001 on tuesday the claim prompted minor government official to quit after admitting signing the pact as congressman one knows how high this goes said adam isacson who monitors colombia for the centre for international policy washingtonbased thinktank senator álvaro araújo who is among the six lawmakers called to testify before the supreme court and is the brother of mr uribes foreign minister maría consuelo araújo warned that if he went down other heads in the government would roll the former head of colombias intelligence agency during mr uribes first term is also being investigated for collusion there is evidence that the president is linked to the paramilitaries in recent speech mr uribe challenged the militia leaders to implicate him if any of those 30000 paramilitaries can say that the president has been complicit let them come forward he said the paramilitaries were formed by wealthy ranchers businessmen and drug mafias in the 80s to fight extortion and kidnapping by leftist guerrillas the paramilitaries later turned into powerful armies involved in drug trafficking and extortion who used their power to control local politics and politicians collusion between the militias and the military and police forces is well documented but the extent to which paramilitaries colluded with politicians and local officials was an open secret while some claims against lawmakers were filed but not pursued more than five years ago the allegations resurfaced after their names were mentioned in files found on the confiscated laptop of top paramilitary leader known as jorge 40 the files allegedly include detailed accounts and recordings of meetings between politicians and paramilitaries to ensure the election of chosen candidates and describe scams in which militias stole state funds from local health services the former militia chiefs are awaiting prosecution under controversial law that grants them reduced prison sentences in
