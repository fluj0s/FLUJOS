through fy2009 to date 36 appropriations authorization or farm disaster acts added approximately 618 billion in supplemental funding for us department of agriculture usda programs excluding the forest 437 billion of the total supplemental funding has been paid directly to farmers primarily through two mechanisms market loss payments 214 billion all from fy1999 to fy2001 to compensate for low farm commodity prices and crop disaster payments 2234 billion paid to any producer who experienced major crop loss caused by natural disaster the remaining 181 billion has funded wide array of other usda programs including livestock feed assistance programs farm conservation programs specialty crop assistance farm loans and nonfarm usda programs such as overseas food aid food and nutrition programs and rural development and housing assistance in recent years virtually all of the supplemental spending has been provided under an emergency designation from congress and the administration meaning that the new spending did not have to be offset with comparable reductions in other programs however in some cases portion of the supplemental is offset by spending reductions in other programs total
