in afghanistan 2008653 isaf quick reaction force responds to ied attack kabul afghanistan november 27 quick reaction force members from the former yugoslav republic of macedonia performed double duties as they worked to ensure safe passage of the visiting nato secretary general jaap hoop scheffer as well as respond to an attack by suicide bomber nov 27 the bomb was detonated from vehicle in massoud square 300 yards from the us embassy building captain saso spasovski commander of the 1st mech infantry brigade and platoon commander for the qrf team said his team was ready to respond to the incident within minutes of the attack our team was especially prepared because of the secretarys visit he said i do not imply the two events are related but because of one event we were instantly prepared for the other sergeant saso nikolovski said responding to an attack leaves room for assumptions as to what to expect when it comes to arriving on the scene one expects to see bad things he said but the kabul city police were quick in responding aiding injured persons and cleaning up the scene so our job was made easy when it came to clearing the area for possible secondary devices spasovksi noted that while on the scene there was great cooperation between the qrf team the afghan national army troops who arrived to assist with security and the kabul city police its unfortunate that these things happen he said but the quick reaction and ability to stabilize the scene showed that afghanistan is capable of picking itself up and for our guys who went out to clear the area its just normal day on the job for them they are trained and prepared to do it again ends kabul afghanistan isaf soldiers from the former yugoslav republic of macedonia patrol kabul oct 1 isaf photo by us
