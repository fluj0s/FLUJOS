dennis zimmerman economics division updated march 8 1994 abstract cigarette excise tax increase of 75 cents per pack has been proposed to ﬁnance part of the presidents universal health care program the tax enjoys considerable public support would raise about 11 billion per year and would be relatively simple to administer because it would increase an existing manufacturers excise tax this report discusses these rationales as well as other eﬀects of and concerns about the tax organized into topics of market failure as justiﬁcation for the tax ie economic eﬃciency potential for revenue equity
